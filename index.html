Hi. I'm James

I make ideas tangible—through code, design, and story. Some became ventures. Some didn’t. A few made money. All taught me something.

My background? Recruitment. Consultancy. Agency work. Headhunting. Advisory. Mixed with new business—because I had to get clients too. Sometimes it was serendipity: a rooftop drink, a missed flight, a conversation that cracked open a market. Other times, it was cold precision: outreach, follow-up, close.

I think it’s called talent acquisition now. I’ve done it globally, across industries and borders. I didn’t just find talent—I attracted it. Through positioning, insight, and conversations that resonated.

I'm aware of the tools recruiters lean on today. They're part of the landscape.

I introduced rare technical specialists into startups, trading floors, and telecom labs—before fintech was a buzzword and MySQL was stable. I didn’t overly rely on just job descriptions. I spoke with the actual hiring managers. I uncovered the real requirements, spoke the language of the role, and drilled deep in interviews. I negotiated salaries, scopes, payment terms. I understood enough about contracts, compliance, and payroll to keep things smooth.

I advised clients on hiring strategy, market positioning, and how to reach the kind of talent that doesn’t respond to job ads. I coached candidates—not formally, but in the way you do when someone’s stuck and one question unlocks everything. I helped them reframe their value, rewrite their narrative, and walk into rooms they didn’t think they belonged in—until they did.
